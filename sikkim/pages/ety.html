ETHINICITY

The majority of Sikkim's residents are Nepali Indians.
The native Sikkimese include the Bhutias, who migrated from the Kham district of Tibet in the 14th century,
and the Lepchas, who are believed to pre-date the Bhutias and are the oldest known inhabitants.
Tibetans reside mostly in the northern and eastern reaches of the state.
Migrant resident communities known as Plainsmen Sikkimese include Bengalis,
Biharis and Marwaris, who are prominent in commerce in South Sikkim and Gangtok,
only those who are the native residents since 1946.
