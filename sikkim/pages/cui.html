CUISINE

Noodle-based dishes such as thukpa, chow mein, thenthuk, fakthu, gyathuk and
wonton are common in Sikkim. Momos – steamed dumplings filled with vegetables,
chicken, mutton, beef or pork and served with soup – are a popular snack.

Beer, whiskey, rum and brandy are widely consumed in Sikkim, as is tongba,
a millet-based alcoholic beverage that is popular in Nepal and Darjeeling.
Sikkim has the third-highest per capita alcoholism rate amongst all Indian states, behind Punjab and Haryana.
